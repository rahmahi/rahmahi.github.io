About Me

I am a theoretical physicist specializing in quantum statistical mechanics and many-body physics. My research focuses on the intersection of quantum localization phenomena, periodically driven systems, and exotic phases of matter. I work extensively with tensor network methods, quantum simulation, and computational approaches to understand complex quantum systems.

Currently, I am exploring quantum neural networks and their applications in many-body physics, alongside investigating novel phenomena such as quantum scars, time crystals, and quantum chimera states. My work bridges theoretical understanding with computational implementation, utilizing advanced numerical methods and high-performance computing resources.

I am passionate about the intersection of quantum physics and emerging technologies, particularly in quantum computing and machine learning applications. My research contributes to understanding fundamental quantum phenomena while exploring their potential technological applications.
